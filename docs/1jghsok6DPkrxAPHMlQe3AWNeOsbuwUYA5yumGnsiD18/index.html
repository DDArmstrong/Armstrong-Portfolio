Sentiment analysis

Who to avoid on online dating sites

By Dan Armstrong

Skills: Python, Pandas, NLTK (Natural Language Toolkit), SentimentIntensityAnalyzer, Datawrapper

I wanted to see whether I could predict the characteristics of men and women on dating sites by applying sentiment analysis to their profiles.

At https://www.kaggle.com/andrewmvd/okcupid-profiles , Kaggle has posted an anonymized dataset of about 60,000 OK Cupid profiles scraped from a Bay Area IP address. I pulled the data into Pandas and explored the demographics, including age, sexual orientation, height, body type, diet, income, education, religion, ethnicity, and use of alcohol, drugs and tobacco.

I also pulled together everything that each person had written about himself or herself and wrote a Python program to analyze it using SentimentIntensityAnalyzer, an NLTK library that scores the text’s positivity or negativity.

Some surprising results:

  • People who wrote a lot about themselves tended to have high negatives - over three times the level of their positives. People who wrote a lot tended to use a lot of “I” statements (self-directed) relative to the number of “you” statements (other-directed). The older the prospective dater, the more positive the sentiment.
